I'm a Scientific Associate at [D.E. Shaw Research](https://www.deshawresearch.com) ("DESRES"). Before that, I was an undergraduate at [Stanford University](https://www.stanford.edu). In 2017, I graduated with a B.S. in Mathematical and Computational Science and an M.S. in Statistics. While there, I was lucky enough to be advised by [Brad Efron](http://statweb.stanford.edu/~ckirby/brad/) and worked on projects involving the oral microbiome and card shuffling with [Susan Holmes](http://statweb.stanford.edu/~susan/susan_person.html) and [Persi Diaconis](https://statweb.stanford.edu/~cgates/PERSI/).

Since 2017, I've been working on improving force fields for biomolecular simulation. With the goal of developing a highly accurate polarizable force field for molecular dynamics simulation, I've worked on the following projects:
* An application of the fused LASSO to atom type selection for small-molecule force fields
* An asynchronous, stochastic second-order optimization method for nonlinear least-squares problems
* A generalization bound for bi-level optimization, as well as the development of an efficient algorithm for minimizing said bound
